How Fat Are We?

A while back I was looking at the statistic that if you collected all the gold ever mined in the world, it would be a cube of side length 20m. I had a really gross thought on the subway the other day:

How much excess fat is there in the world? Like, if you could group all the excess fat into a big blob, how big would it be?

This question got under my skin (har har), so I set out to figure it out. I started (and finished) with Canada, because I'm from Canada.

Some Data

After digging around the Stats Canada page for a while and not finding much that was very helpful (read: the Stats Canada site is pretty trash), I found this page, which has self-reported obesity and overweight data. The data's from 2014 for most Canadian adults, which is pretty decent. Pen, meet back of the envelope. First, the numbers:

  • If you took all the excess body mass in Canadian adults, it would be a cube with side length 58 metres!
  • To put that in more Canadian terms, you could fill every indoor ice rink in Canada to a depth of almost 5 centimetres with excess body mass!
  • That's gross!
