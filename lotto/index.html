Lotto Max Analysis

I have a confession to make: I'm not immune to the allure of the lottery. There are typically two times when I'll play the lottery:

  • When I'm really bummed out about work
  • When the Lotto Max jackpot is higher than $50 million (now $60).

If you're not familiar with the Lotto Max, here's how it's structured:

  • It's active across all of Canada
  • Players pay $5 to pick 7 numbers, and if your numbers all match you win the jackpot (you know, like... a lottery)
  • There's a minimum jackpot of $10 million, and a max jackpot of $50 million (changed to $60 million in July 2015)
  • Any integer multiples of $1 million in the jackpot over $50 million are given out separately as "MaxMillions" - with a separate draw for each one
  • All winnings are paid out as a tax-free lump sum (#OhCanada)
  • I've never won*
