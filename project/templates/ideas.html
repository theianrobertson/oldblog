Ideas

A while back I was recently reading a post on Hacker News pointing to a page talking about "open-sourcing*" one's ideas for businesses (and smaller ideas). The thought being two-fold:

  1. Just having an idea isn't worth much, if at all. Something like 99.9% of what makes a successful company comes in the execution, not just the idea
  2. Most of your ideas come out of a need you've experienced yourself. If someone else takes the idea and runs with it, you may wind up on the winning end anyway, because your pain point could be solved.

I also read a good opinion piece about "Idea Debt", i.e. when you waste your time thinking too much about the future instead of getting things done.

As such, I figured I'd share out ideas that have been bumping around in my head for a while, so that either a) someone else can run with it and I'll benefit, or at least b) I can write it down and move on to other things. Here's my list**:

The Forgetful Gatekeeper

A tool to lock yourself out of a password-accessed website or program. Here's my thought:

  • You enter a passphrase, and a date you want to be locked out until
  • A password is generated and copied to your clipboard for you
  • You paste the generated password in to the "new password" field of a site (let's say: Facebook)
  • The tool won't give you the password back until you hit the date, and re-enter your passphrase.

Obviously this wouldn't stand up to password resets by email/hints, but it could be cool for when you really want to focus for a while, or kick a habit cold turkey

SKU-level receipt data

Some company is going to solve this - access to customers and companies to item-level receipt data. I feel like this is something that Mastercard/Visa will eventually figure out, where there could be a model like: Retailer provides item-level info in exchange for the ability to push promotional material, credit/debit issuer gets item-level info for analytics. Benefits are:

  • I can budget more granularly (esp. at the grocery store)
  • If I want to track the price I'm paying for something over time, I could easily extract that info (For me it'd be something silly like I'd want to track how much my peanut butter bill on a month-to-month basis)

Talk to Me

This idea isn't full fleshed out and I think there may be two separate things here, but here's what I'm thinking about: I live and interact in a little bubble, wherein most people are pro-choice, believe in global warming, left-leaning, upper-middle class, relatively intelligent, etc... I'm also a guy that knows very little about what's happening in Syria, or Crimea, or France, or Australia. What if I could go online and find someone that wants to talk on the other side of an issue? Like I say "Hi, I'd like to talk to someone that thinks everyone should be able to get a gun", and they match me up. Then we could just chat about the different sides of the issue.

FeeFieFiddly.io

Start a website for banjo enthusiasts and call it Fee-Fie-Fiddly.io. That's... that's it.

Delay the Internet

If I'm watching football on PVR, I would love to be able to follow my fantasy scores in "non-real time", i.e. if I'm watching the 1:00 game at 4:00, I want my fantasy scores to only show me what's happened up until that point. Ditto my Twitter feed. Otherwise the results are spoiled!
