The Fear Boners

So I play in a fantasy football league with some friends, which is called "The Fear Boners", after the episode of The League. In the short (four year) history of the league, I've now won TWICE (eat it), which is approximately 50% of the seasons, by my calculations.

Year Team Name Place
2012 Arian Nation 1 of 8
2013 Johnsons & Johnsons 6 of 8
2014 Mom's Spaghetti 7 of 8
2015 #1 Dad 1 of 8

Back in 2012 I kicked ass - I won the first overall draft pick by winning a punt/pass/kick competition, came first overall in the season, and won the playoffs. This year my team was pretty piss-poor, and I wound up with the fewest points for of any team in the league. After thanking the fantasy football gods, I started wondering just how big of a fluke it was that I had won this year. Fired up some Python and set to work figuring it out:

I had no business making the playoffs this year.

Since the teams were randomly seeded against each other, I could just use the same game schedule, but switch up which teams were assigned to which "team number". Since there's 8 teams, there were 40,320 (8P8) possible combinations.

I scored out all of the games in each of those seasons, and figured out the final standings. We have an unnecessarily complex tie-breaker (number of weekly wins, then head-to-head record, then total points for), so that added some more wrinkles to the calculation.

In our league the top 4 teams make the playoffs, and the bottom 4 teams make the "Sacko" playoffs, i.e. they compete to not come in last place. I was only supposed to finish in the top 4 teams in 5.5% of the seasons!

And I should have done slightly worse in the playoffs

If I run my simulations out to the end of the playoffs (weeks 14-16), here's the percent of seasons won/lost by each team:

Dang, The Brown Notes could have been the champs in almost 70% of season iterations. And they didn't even make the playoffs in real life!

So what?

Here's what I'm taking away from this analysis:

  • I had no business making the playoffs
  • The Brown Notes are probably real pissed off at me
  • I'm reeeeeeally good at fantasy football

Note: I can't guarantee that everyone started the players they would have started normally in the playoffs, due to how byes worked. I'm just not going to consider that, so the regular season stuff is probably a bit more accurate than the playoff stuff. Also I assume some people try to play a matchup with the fantasy team their playing against, so that could change things week-to-week too. I don't do that though, and I won the league so there.
